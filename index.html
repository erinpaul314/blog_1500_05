Why is Mark Zuckerberg's Metaverse Failing?

https://news.northeastern.edu/2022/11/03/metaverse-failure/ November 11, 2022

Despite spending billions of dollars on the Metaverse, Mark Zuckerberg’s virtual reality endeavor is failing. Celia Pearce, an associate professor of game design at Northeastern, describes virtual worlds of the past, such as “Second Life” and explains why they succeeded where Metaverse failed. One of the major reasons is the apparent lack of innovation beyond the virtual worlds of the 2000s. The failure has contributed to Meta’s stock falling 70%, which was followed to the decision to lay off 11,0000 of their employees. However, Zuckerberg and Meta are continuing to invest in the Metaverse, committing another $15 billion to its development. Other factors contributing to the Metaverse’s struggles are its lack of aesthetic appeal in comparison to modern video games, lack of market research into what consumers want, and its confusion regarding a target audience. However, the most glaring issue is simple: The Metaverse is more an abstract concept than a product, and without a clear distinction of what consumers will be getting, it’s an impossible idea to sell. Given the list of issues, it’s easy to assume the Metaverse will never work. But Pearce asserts that at its core concept, it is something people want, which is evident in the community built around “Second Life” 20 years ago. Given this fact, coupled with the team and resources available to Zuckerberg, it’s almost shocking to see the complete failure that is the Metaverse unfold in real time, and to think of the wasted potential.
