Why is Mark Zuckerberg's Metaverse Failing?


Metaverse Failure

November 11, 2022

Despite spending billions of dollars on the Metaverse, Mark Zuckerberg’s virtual reality endeavor is failing. Celia Pearce, an associate professor of game design at Northeastern, describes virtual worlds of the past, such as “Second Life” and explains why they succeeded where Metaverse failed. One of the major reasons is the apparent lack of innovation beyond the virtual worlds of the 2000s. The failure has contributed to Meta’s stock falling 70%, which was followed to the decision to lay off 11,000 of their employees. However, Zuckerberg and Meta are continuing to invest in the Metaverse, committing another $15 billion to its development. Other factors contributing to the Metaverse’s struggles are its lack of aesthetic appeal in comparison to modern video games, lack of market research into what consumers want, and its confusion regarding a target audience. However, the most glaring issue is simple: The Metaverse is more an abstract concept than a product, and without a clear distinction of what consumers will be getting, it’s an impossible idea to sell. Given the list of issues, it’s easy to assume the Metaverse will never work. But Pearce asserts that at its core concept, it is something people want, which is evident in the community built around “Second Life” 20 years ago. Given this fact, coupled with the team and resources available to Zuckerberg, it’s almost shocking to see the complete failure that is the Metaverse unfold in real time, and to think of the wasted potential.

Germany bets on global green hydrogen economy


Germany Bets on Global Green Hydrogen Economy

November 17, 2022

To help develop a global green hydrogen economy, Germany has announced that they will contribute €550 million to fund the technology. Countries such as South Africa plan to produce 12 million tons of green hydrogen annually by 2050, and half of this will be exported to nearby countries to aid in transitioning away from fossil fuels. Green hydrogen is produced via electrolysis. Water molecules are split into oxygen and hydrogen, and the hydrogen is then extracted using renewable energy, not fossil fuels, and therefore produces no carbon dioxide. As countries like the USA and Germany strive for net-zero carbon emissions, production is expected to grow to 400 million tons by 2050. Southern countries with high potential for solar power production could be leading the way in green hydrogen as they have a year-round renewable energy source.

Canadian technology leaders plan to invest heavily in major emerging technologies


Canadian Technology Leaders Plan to Invest in Emerging Technologies

November 24, 2022

More Canadian companies are investing in emerging technologies, including Web3, 5G, edge computing, quantum computing, and virtual realities. These investments are among both public and private sectors, and KPMG Sanjay Park says "The race to transform is on," says Sanjay Pathak, Partner and National Leader, Technology Strategy and Digital Transformation Services, KPMG in Canada. "At a time when digital leaders face rising costs, a potential recession, and a global talent crisis, they are turning to emerging technologies to build business resiliency, harness data and analytics to enhance decision making, and drive growth." Despite a looming recession, these companies believe investing in these technologies will make the difference in years to come. However, many programs in the tech sector have been paused due to preparations for the recession such as layoffs, as well as burnout and lack of skilled workers. Another barrier in these programs is the reluctance of companies in letting go of the past. This only limits their growth, as digital transformations have proven to increase profits and performance at these organizations.

Did Physicists Create a Wormhole in a Quantum Computer?


Did Physicists Create a Wormhole in a Quantum Computer?

December 9, 2022

Using quantum computing, physicists have simulated a virtual wormhole, allowing to transport quantume states between two locations. These experiments could aid in quantum theory within abstract universes, a problem that physicists are unable to debunk for our own universe. Wormholes are passages through space time that connect centers of blackholes, and were first proposed by Albert Einstein and Nathan Rosen in 1935. Einstein's general theory of relativity purports that wormholes are allowed. However the wormholes in virtual universes, such as that in the expierment, would not be able to exist in our universe. Still, these wormholes can 'teleport' quantum information from one place to another. This experiement was first imagined when the physcis of 'exotic universes' was linked to virtual quantum universes with more typical conditions. This research, however, is divisive, as some view it as a pointless dead end, while others see it as a legitmate path to developing quantum theory in our own universe.
